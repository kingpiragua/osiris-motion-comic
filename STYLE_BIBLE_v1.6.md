## 2. THE TWO-SIGNAL COLOR SYSTEM (locked v1.3–v1.4)

Color is not decoration. **Color is the emotional and factional grammar of the universe.** The palette tells the reader which force is present before they read a single caption.

### OSIRIS — GREEN (signal / memory / awe)
- Warm phosphor green, **contained inside its source** (the CRT screen)
- **Green core fading to amber at the edges** — real CRT bloom physics, NOT pure #00FF00
- Green appears where the signal is present and **recedes where it isn't**
- Room breathes in amber, sandstone, bronze, deep indigo shadow
- Emotional register: **awe, wonder, arrival, memory, the sacred**

### NETWORK — RED (surveillance / threat / dread)
- Cold oxblood, crimson, deep maroon — **muted and matte, never candy-red or neon**
- **Ambient saturation** — red soaks the whole room; the Network is *everywhere*, already compromising the space, watching from every symbol
- No warm amber falloff; the room is colder, more hostile
- Emotional register: **dread, surveillance, threat, the room is already lost**

> **CANON NOTE (RESOLVED v1.6):** Network red is **ambient saturation** (Option A) — red soaks the whole room; the Network is everywhere, already compromising the space. This is now canon. It reads as more distinct from green and more thematically true to a surveillance antagonist. Confirmed on Page 5, Panel 4.

### DISCIPLINE RULE
Never let both signals dominate one panel without narrative reason. Never let green creep out of its source in an OSIRIS panel. Never let red go garish. The restraint is the whole point.

## 3. RENDERING & LINEWORK (locked v1.2–v1.3)

- **Linework:** nervous hand-inked, variable weight, slightly imperfect (Matsumoto / Inoue / Takehiko Inoue tremor). It must look *drawn*, not rendered.
- **Ink weight:** heavy foreground blacks; line drops out in lit areas.
- **Texture:** matte, printed, risograph-adjacent. Visible halftone dots and CRT scanlines.
- **Kill the gloss.** If it looks like polished concept art, it is wrong.
- **Lighting:** single-source CRT bloom. Faces lit from below/side by screens. Deep noir shadow, high contrast.

---

## 4. CAMERA LANGUAGE (proven range — v1.3, v1.5)

Vary the camera every panel. Standing directive: no two consecutive panels share an angle. Proven range that holds the style:

- **Worm's-eye / low-angle** — scale + dread; the room curves over the figure like a tomb ceiling (Nihei). Signature move.
- **Bird's-eye / high-angle** — looking down; top of curly hair prominent.
- **Strict profile** — proven to hold Skye's face; use freely.
- **Macro** — eyes + glasses only; screen-text reflected in lenses (recurring device — the screen infecting the person, Satoshi Kon).
- **Wide / distant** — figure small in cluttered room; environmental scale.
- **Action** — reaching, motion, hair in movement, dynamic angle.

### CANONICAL DEVICES (proven, reusable signature beats)
- **Lens-reflection macro** — screen text (green or red) reflected across the glasses with the eyes readable beneath. The screen infecting the person (Satoshi Kon). Established v1.5.
- **The Split-Eye** (Page 5) — in a two-signal collision, render her two eyes in *different signals*: one lens green, one lens red, the seam down the nose. She is the site where OSIRIS and the Network fight for her. **Iconic franchise beat — use sparingly, only at true collision moments.**

## 4b. THE GREEN→RED HANDOFF (proven v1.6, Page 5)

When a page turns from OSIRIS to Network within a single page, do it as a **hard cut, not a gradual blend.**

- Green owns the top panels (awe, the signal). Red owns the bottom panels (dread, surveillance).
- **One hinge panel** carries the snap — ideally the Split-Eye macro, warm on one edge, cold overtaking.
- **Never average the palette evenly across all panels** — that produces muddy amber-red and kills the emotional turn. The snap *is* the horror.
- The same line of dialogue can flip meaning across the turn (e.g. "they don't know who's watching back" = defiance in green, dread in red). Exploit this.

## 5. SYMBOL LANGUAGE

Egyptian + Taíno + Boricua + circuit motifs. Dense, but must **radiate from a light source** (Aaron Douglas geometry / halo effect) rather than float as flat wallpaper. In worm's-eye framing, symbols press down from the ceiling, haloing the figure in real perspective.

Recurring anchors: Eye of Horus / winged scarab sunburst · ankh (necklace + wall) · coquí + Taíno frog · "NUNCA OLVIDES DE DÓNDE VIENES" · "LA VERDAD ESTÁ EN LA MEMORIA" · Paseo Boricua / Humboldt Park signage · Puerto Rican flag · DISK tag glyph · circuit-eye franchise mark.

### GEOGRAPHIC ACCURACY (canon — do not violate)
- **Paseo Boricua = Division Street.** The true icon is the giant arching **steel Puerto Rican flag sculpture** spanning the street (one at each end of the strip). This is THE landmark of the cold open, not a generic skyline.
- **No elevated L train on Division Street.** Do not place elevated tracks/trains on Paseo Boricua. (The corpus Image 4 L-train was a geography error — do not repeat.) Rain-slick brick streets, low-rise brick tenements, storefront signage, streetlights reflecting on wet asphalt is the correct vernacular.
- Real neighborhood texture beats invented cyberpunk skyline. Ground the futurism in the actual barrio.
