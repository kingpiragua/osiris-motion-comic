# 01 · Canon Bible

The rules of what is true in OSIRIS.EXE, and the tone everything must serve.

## Logline

A signal buried in Humboldt Park — a Puerto Rican lineage, a code that refuses to
be erased, and an archive that remembers what the city tried to forget.

## Premise

Before OSIRIS was a system, he was a boy. Memory, lineage, and resistance are
encoded as a literal *signal* that survives every attempt to delete it. The story
runs the way the world runs: it doesn't wait for you — it auto-advances.

## Core themes

- **Memory as resistance** — *"Justicia sin memoria es control."*
- **Erasure vs. the archive** — every system tries to delete; the archive denies.
- **Lineage as code** — a bloodline becomes a protocol; the father is "archived."
- **The mask as freedom** — surveillance turned into a route out.
- **Decolonial myth** — Boricua history fused with Egyptian death-myth.

## Canon law (non-negotiables)

1. **The archive cannot be permanently erased.** It can be split, hidden,
   corrupted, or compromised — never deleted. (`ERASE: DENIED`.)
2. **The signal precedes Frank.** It "started" before him — the second-signal
   twist. Do not write the signal as originating with a single person.
3. **Color is law.** Green = archive alive / Boricua signal. Red = compromised /
   Pale Horse / the law. See [Symbol Dictionary](05-symbol-dictionary.md).
4. **Character look rules are fixed.** Skye: round glasses + curly hair down,
   every appearance. (See per-character notes in [Characters](03-characters.md).)
5. **History is real.** Vieques, Guánica 1898, the Young Lords, FALN are actual
   history, not invented set-dressing. Treat with accuracy.

## Tone

Retro green-phosphor terminal horror. Analog haunting a digital world. Bilingual
(English / Spanish) without translation crutches. Reverent toward the history,
unsettling toward the system.
