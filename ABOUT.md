# About

## OSIRIS.EXE // Pale Horse Protocol

A signal buried in Humboldt Park, Chicago — a Puerto Rican lineage, a code that
refuses to be erased, and an archive that remembers what the city tried to
forget. It plays like a comic that runs itself: 115 hand-curated panels that
auto-advance through a retro green-phosphor terminal — scanlines, glitch, CRT
power-on, and a slow drift on every panel.

**▶ Play it live:** https://kingpiragua.github.io/osiris-motion-comic/

## About the artist

**Disk Darián** (kingpiragua) is a Puerto Rican artist and storyteller working
at the intersection of diaspora history and speculative horror.

His work fuses Boricua resistance — the Young Lords, Vieques, Guánica 1898, the
streets of Humboldt Park — with Egyptian death-myth and a retro-tech CRT
aesthetic, building stories that read like an archive that refuses to be deleted.
Vejigante masks meet Giger godforms; a bloodline becomes a protocol.

His flagship work, **OSIRIS.EXE // Pale Horse Protocol**, is a 115-panel
self-running motion comic delivered as a single dependency-free web experience
that plays on any device, online or offline.

> *"Justicia sin memoria es control."*

## The niche

Decolonial Puerto Rican mythology as living code — where the Young Lords meet
Osiris in a haunted terminal. It sits where most creators don't overlap:

- **Puerto Rican / Nuyorican lineage & resistance** — Humboldt Park, the Young
  Lords, Vieques '99, Guánica 1898, FALN. *Ser boricua, ni olvido, pa'lante.*
- **Egyptian mythology rewired as code** — Osiris / Duat / the Pale Horse
  Protocol; resurrection reframed as a signal that refuses to be erased.
- **Vejigante / mask culture** — the *enmascarado* and *vejigante* threading folk
  tradition into the horror-mythos.
- **Cyber-horror aesthetic** — Giger-style godforms, glitch, surveillance city,
  dead phones, "the archive remembers."
- **Format as part of the art** — a single-file, dependency-free, self-running
  motion comic. The medium *is* the message: an archive that runs itself.

**One line:** *Decolonial Puerto Rican mythology as living code — where the
Young Lords meet Osiris in a haunted terminal.*
